Below is the timeline of President Barack Obama:
1961

Birth

Obama was born in Honolulu, Hawaii to Mr Barack Obama Snr and Ann Dunham.

1967-1985

Education

He schooled in Indonesia attending Elementary school and was home schooled at a time also from 1967 to 1971 when he returned to Honolulu and chose to live with his grandparents. He graduated from high school in 1979. He moved to Los Angeles where he attended Occidental College till 1981 and transferred to Columbia University, New York as a junior. He graduated in 1983 with a BA in Political Science with specialty in International Relations. After graduation, he worked for Business International Corporation and later for New York Public Interest Research Group till 1985.

1985-1997

Law Career

He worked as a Community Organizer from June 1985 to May 1988 in Chicago. In the fall of 1988 he entered Harvard Law School and graduated with a Juris Doctor degree magna cum laude in 1991 and returned to Chicago afterwards. In 1991, Obama accepted a two-year position as Visiting Law and Government Fellow at the University of Chicago Law School to work on his first book. He then taught constitutional law at the University of Chicago Law School for twelve years, first as a Lecturer from 1992 to 1996, and then as a Senior Lecturer from 1996 to 2004. Obama's law license became inactive in 2007.

1997-2008

Legislative Career

His political career started in 1996 when he was elected into Illinios State Senate as a Democrat. He was reelected in 1998 and 2002. However, in 2000 he lost Democratic primary race or Illinois's 1st congressional district in the United States House of Representatives to four-term incumbent Bobby Rush by a margin of two to one. He got elected into US Senate in 2004 and therefore resigned from his position as Illinios State Senator. His stint in the US Senate was a short one as he already had his eyes on the number one seat. He resigned his position on November 16, 2008 like he earlier announced.

2008-2017

Presidency

Obama announced his candidacy for President of the United States on January 10, 2007 and on November 4, 2008Obama won the presidency with 365 electoral votes to 173 received by McCain.[175] Obama won 52.9% of the popular vote to McCain's 45.7%. He became the first African American to be elected President of the United States of America and he served out his two-terms after defeating Republican Mitt Romney on November 6, 2012. On January 20, 2017 he handed over the mantle of leadership to incumbent President Donald Trump.
